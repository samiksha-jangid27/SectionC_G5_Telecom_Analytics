## Business Problem

The Indian telecom sector faces significant customer churn, where subscribers discontinue service due to competitive pricing, poor service quality, network coverage gaps, and availability of better alternatives. This attrition directly impacts revenue stability and customer lifetime value. Understanding which customer segments are most vulnerable and what behavioral and demographic factors drive their decisions is critical for designing retention strategies that prevent revenue leakage and improve long-term business profitability.

**Core Business Question**

> Which customer segments are most likely to churn in the Indian telecom sector, what behavioral and demographic factors drive this churn, and how can telecom companies proactively reduce customer attrition?

**Decision Supported**

> This analysis enables telecom companies to identify high-risk customer segments and implement targeted retention strategies such as personalized plans, pricing adjustments, and service improvements, thereby reducing churn and minimizing potential revenue loss.
